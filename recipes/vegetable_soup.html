Vegetable Soup Recipes

The African vegetable soup is one of our favorite meals in Africa. It is normally prepared with some combination fo greenish vegetables: fluted pumpkin leave locally known as "Ugu leave", "Eru leave" wild spinach locally known as Afang leave, dandelion green leave locally known as "Efo Yamrin", beef or chicken, dried fish or stock fish, and a host of other ingredients. Its usually greenish brown in color, depending on the way the chef prepares it and a taste of it is guaranteed to be one of the most savory and delicious spinach recipes you will ever taste. Most people consume it with some form of solid moulded lump of cereal commonly known as "gary" or "fufu", though its more tasty when it is eaten with grain like rice. This Nigerian vegetable stew smoothies is best prepared and consumed within a day; there should be no left over for the next day. Whenever tourish visit the continent of Africa, its one of the best food they would love to introduced to their families when they go back home.

Ingredients

  • Chopped spinach
  • Cooked meat of your choice; I prefer Chicken
  • Dried fish
  • Stock fish
  • Some large size tomatoes
  • A large size of onion
  • Gloves of garlic
  • Salt
  • Some seed of pepper
  • 4 teaspoon of powdered dried shrimp
  • A cup of palm oil
  • Hebanero peppers

Steps

  1. To begin the preparation of this sumptuous African recipes, blend half of the onion with the tomatoes, bell pepper, garlic and hot watear. Chop the other half of the red onion.
  2. In a deep sauce pan or stock pot, heat the oil and saute the onion over medium meat until it turns golden brown.
  3. Add the blended tomatoes and allow to cook over medium heat for about 30 minutes. Stir occasionally to avoid the mix from burning.
  4. After 30 minutes, the sauce should have significantly reduced, at this point season it with powdered dried shrimp and some salt.
  5. You should be adding your beef or chicken at this point.
  6. Add the fresh shrimp to the sauce, then the spinach.
  7. Gently stir the stew and the spinach until the spinach is wilted completely.
  8. At this point, you should be adding your preseasoned fish or boiled stock fish.
  9. Finally, you can now add some extra seasoning - salt for taste, and other spices; then allow the stew to steam for a while. "Whoa la", your dish is ready to be served!
